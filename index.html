World Cup Scorers — by Opponent Strength

For each top scorer, how strong were the teams they scored against? Strength is the opponent's World Football Elo, frozen before each tournament. The metric describes who a player happened to face — shaped by the draw and the knockout structure as much as the player.

Tiers: each tournament's qualified field is ranked by Elo; the top quarter is elite, then strong, mid, weak. Elo as of Dec 31 of the year before the tournament. Bar length is proportional to goals, so a longer bar means more goals.

How to read this

This page measures, for the World Cup's top scorers, how strong the teams they scored against were — by each opponent's World Football Elo. It describes which opponents a player happened to face, which is shaped by the draw and the knockout structure as much as by the player. Every term below is defined once here; metric labels elsewhere link back to it.

Elo — World Football Elo
A single team-strength number, chess-style: every result moves it, adjusted for goal margin, match importance and home advantage. From eloratings.net. Studies have found Elo-based ratings predict results better than the old FIFA ranking (Lasek et al., 2013); FIFA itself adopted an Elo-based method in 2018.
Frozen pre-tournament
Each opponent's Elo is taken as of Dec 31 of the year before the tournament, so the tournament's own results can't contaminate the rating.
Tier / percentile
Each tournament's qualified field is ranked by frozen Elo; the top quarter is elite, then strong, mid, weak. Tiers are relative to that tournament's field — era-fair, so a 16-team and a 48-team edition compare fairly.
Elite share
Share of a player's goals scored against elite (top-quarter) opponents. One stat among several, and boundary-sensitive — see the * mark.
Avg opp Elo
Mean frozen Elo of the teams a player scored against — raw and era-revealing (not adjusted across eras). The companion lens to elite share.
The * and † marks
* — a goal sits within ~3.5 Elo of a tier boundary, so that one goal's tier hinges on a near-tie (the percentage is exact; that single input is fragile). † — a cross-era comparison; read elite share alongside absolute Elo.
